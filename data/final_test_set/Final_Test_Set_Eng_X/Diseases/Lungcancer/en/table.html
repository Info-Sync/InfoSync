Other namesLung carcinoma
SpecialtyOncology, pulmonology
SymptomsCoughing (including coughing up blood), weight loss, shortness of breath, chest pains
Usual onset~70 years
TypesSmall-cell lung carcinoma (SCLC), non-small-cell lung carcinoma (NSCLC)
Risk factorsTobacco smoking | genetic factors | radon gas | asbestos | air pollution
Diagnostic methodMedical imaging, tissue biopsy
PreventionNot smoking, avoiding radon gas, asbestos, second-hand smoke, or other forms of air pollution exposure.
TreatmentSurgery, chemotherapy, radiotherapy
PrognosisFive-year survival rate: 10 to 20% (most countries), 33% (Japan), 27% (Israel), 25% (Republic of Korea)
Frequency3.3 million affected as of 2015
Deaths1.8 million (2020)
